Frequently Asked Questions

How much does using Parachute cost my facility?

Parachute is free for facilities to use.

Do I still need paper scripts and therapy notes?

No. When placing an order in Parachute, you’ll answer a few insurance qualifying questions directly on the platform in place of separate documentation.

By selecting the product and answering basic questions about medical necessity, Parachute will generate the WOPD (script), face-to-face, and, when applicable, the CMN that the MD/PA/NP will sign.

Is Parachute Health HIPAA compliant?

Yes. Parachute Health is compliant with HIPAA guidelines and completes an annual HIPAA compliance audit to protect your patients’ data.

Can multiple people in my facility use Parachute?

Yes. Your facility can have unlimited users. There are two roles: “Admin” users (who can place DME orders and manage users) and “Standard” users (who can place orders only). Admin users must be director level or above (e.g., Director of Social Services).

Can orders be signed digitally?

Yes. Clinicians can sign orders via text message or email without creating a Parachute account.

On first use, the MD/NP/PA confirms their identity and NPI. After that, they receive a secure link to Parachute’s HIPAA-compliant site where they can approve orders with a single click, keeping the process fully paperless.

What if my MD/NP/PA prefers not to e-sign?

Parachute allows you to print an order, obtain a handwritten signature, and upload the signed order back into Parachute. You can also fax the script, but faxed orders typically take longer to be signed than digital ones.

Can I submit one order for multiple pieces of DME?

Yes. You can include as many items as needed in a single order for the same patient.

Can my facility share one generic login?

No. For HIPAA compliance, Parachute must know which individual placed each order, so generic/shared logins are not permitted.

Does Parachute cover all insurances?

Parachute is an ordering platform, not a supplier. You can use any insurance that is accepted by the supplier you’re ordering from—in this case, Choice Medical Equipment.
